# Publication Review Policy

No analysis or model response is automatically publishable.

A research release must identify:

- the approved source registrations and immutable dataset snapshots;
- the preregistered plan and deviations;
- code revision, environment, parameters, and executed runs;
- tables, figures, effect sizes, uncertainty, and null results;
- external evidence, contradictory evidence, and limitations;
- required dataset acknowledgements and source attribution;
- authorship, conflicts, funding, and disclosures; and
- scientific and publication approvals.

Every substantive sentence must trace to an executed artifact, an external
source, or a clearly labeled interpretation. Source publication permission and
attribution requirements are enforced before release.

## Founder-led review and publication status

NaS is currently founder-led. Dalron J. Robertson may perform and authorize a
documented internal review after completing the required scientific,
statistical, governance, reproducibility, and claim checks. That review is
labeled `internal_self_review`, never independent review or peer review.

AI assistance is labeled `ai_assisted_internal_review`. It may identify issues
and propose changes but cannot authorize a gate, approve publication, or be
represented as a human reviewer.

Qualified external experts may review a near-final release or manuscript. Their
feedback is recorded separately and is not called journal peer review. Only a
journal-administered process may change publication status to peer reviewed.

Before formal journal peer review, every public NaS paper must visibly state
that it is a founder-led research report or preprint and has not yet undergone
formal independent journal peer review. See
[`../solo-research-operating-model.md`](../solo-research-operating-model.md).

Material corrections create a new visible version. Silent substantive edits,
fabricated reviewers, undisclosed AI approval, and retroactive claims of
independence are prohibited.
